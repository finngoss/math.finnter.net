2. Visibility of Unimodular Integral Lattices Λ⊆Zn

An m dimensional Unimodular Lattice is a lattice generated by the columns of an nxm matrix whose fundamental parallelotope has m-dimensional volume 1. This is the sqrt of the Gramian M⊤M.

2.1 Visibility of Zn from any point x∈Zn

Introduces the general idea of “visible from any point” rather than just “visible from the origin”. It’s mentioned sometimes in the literature (simultaneous visibility paper) but not very common.

Anyways, this is just a jumping off point for me to begin the notion of visibility the way I want it. I can define vocabulary.

From any vantage point in space, one can consider the visibility of objects around them in that same space.

Reprove that visibility of Zn is 1/ζ(n). Prove that visibility from any other point is also 1/ζ(n)

2.2 Visibility of Zm×{0}n−m from any point x∈Zn

This is the visibility of affine subspaces
For m=n-1, this is the visibility of a hyperplane (from the origin) after a translation by x. I haven’t added in the SLnZ stuff yet, that will come (unimodular!)

I DONT understand the cases m\neq n-1… but I bet I can figure them outtttt <3

2.3 Visibility of Zn and Zm×Z≥0n−m​ from any point x∈Zm×{0}n−m

Dual to 2.2.

This is sort of a dual notion of visibility. Rather than thinking extrinsically, like we’re viewing a space that has been embedded into our space, we can think intrinsically. From within an embedded space, what is the visibility of the ambient space? The two cases I gave should have equal density because of symmetry? but I honestly dont know!

Related to the history of visible lattice points with Olber’s paradox! <3

2.4 Visibility of Λ⊆Zn generated by a matrix M=UInxm​V for U∈SLn​Z,V∈SLm​Z from any point x∈Zn

A generalization of 2.2.

Here I’m defining Inxm​ to be the column matrix of the first m elementary unit vectors ei​. You can view it as the generating matrix of the lattice Zm⊂Zn

We now move to considering any transformation by unimodular matrices of these unit vectors within their own spanned space as well within the larger ambient space Zn.

I know that for m=n-1 this transformation has no affect on the density! But I should iron it out a little bit for my paper with Kelly.

I suspect it’s the same for m≤n−1 but I’m not positive…

The idea is that the density of lattices doesn’t change as you transform them by SLn​Z, what matters is the point you view them from.

2.5 Visibility of Zn (or any half-space?) from any point x∈Λ⊆Zn generated by a matrix M=UInxm​V for U∈SLn​Z,V∈SLm​Z

Dual to 2.4. A generalization of 2.3.

Given any nxm unimodular matrix, I can describe the density of points visible from a specified point x∈Zn
