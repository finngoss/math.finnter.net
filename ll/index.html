Finnley's Hyperharbor

This hyperlink library is designed and maintained primarily for my own use because I hate browser bookmark bars and dropdown menus in general. However, you might find something interesting here if you poke around! I am affectionately calling it my hyperharbor due to the large number of anchor elements, each linking my site to a different part of the WWW. Before we had "effective" search enginges and page rank, people would navigate the internet through manually maintained and compiled compediums such as this one. In fact, the very first search engine (the W3 Catalog) relied on such intentionally curated lists. I see the advent of "effective" search engines as the beginning of the end of a human-centric web. After GoTo.com introduced paid search results (pay per click) in 1993 for the premier real estate at the top of search engine results, Google and other browsers quickly followed suit. Thus Capitalism became the primary force governing the infrastructure of the web and the way we move through it...

“Ten years ago every web site had a section of external links because people felt it was their personal responsibility to configure the environment and build the infrastructure. The many-to-many principle showed itself in linking strategies as well. A site was not complete without links to other sites.” - Olia Lialina, 2005
