**Given a sublattice $\Lambda\subset\Z^n$, what is the density of visible points on the sublattice?**

Here I'm defining a sublattice to be the span of m linearly independent vectors in $\Z^n, m\leq n$. 

For some fixed $x\in\Z^n$, a lattice point $y$ is said to be visible from x if and only if $\gcd(y-x)=1$. 

Visibility of $\Lambda$ from $x$ = Density of points in $\Lambda$ that are visible from $x$
- can be defined asymptotically. Let $N(r)=\{y\in\Lambda : ||y||_{\infty} \leq r\}$ and $V(r)=\{y\in\Lambda : ||y||_{\infty} \leq r, \gcd(y-x)=1\}$. The density is defined to be $\lim_{r\to\infty}\frac{|V(r)|}{|N(r)|}$.

Classical Result: Visibility of $\Z^n$ from any point in $\Z^n$ is $\frac{1}{\zeta(n)}$, where $\zeta$
is the Riemann-Zeta function.
