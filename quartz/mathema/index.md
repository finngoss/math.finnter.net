# Something Learned
'Mathema' is the Greek word meaning "something learned" and the root of our word 'mathematics!' I'm sure to encounter many mathemata on my journey towards becoming a mathematician, so I'm beginning this blog as my repository to keep track of anything of interest to me. This may include class notes, takeaways from papers I've read, research questions, etc. I also hope to slowly grow my understanding of various fields of math, their central theories/theorems/objects, and how they relate to each other. 

This blog is first and foremost a tool for my own learning and referencing, though I hope that with time it becomes a robust educational resource for anyone wishing to learn more mathemata.

### Notes on Organization
Because so many of my interests live between disciplines, I have set out to avoid hierarchical (arborescent) organization and opt for a more distributed (rhizomatic) system of file relations. As such, the best way to bounce around this blog is by following hyperlinks from one post to another! Some information may get repetitive, but that's how meaning is manifested. 

The folders I do use, however, are:
- Course Notes: for keeping track of notes of explicit courses I have taken/am taking
- People: for identifying important (or at the very least accessible) figures in various areas.


I also make ample use of tags, toggleable in the graph view. While no categorization can capture every nuance, I find it helpful to have an imbrication of relations and kaleidoscopic lenses through which to make sense of things. Currently, the tags I use are:
- Field: for the broad, general, sides of modern mathematics (e.g. algebra, analysis, number theory). Each field is also its own tag.
- Area: for broad assemblages of theories and problem solving techniques, existing under or between fields. 
- Theory: for slightly more specialized approaches or classes of problems.
- Technique: for a useful strategy in an area or for solving a specific type of problem
- Problem: for a very specific problem
- Object: entities of mathematical interest
