

I began by studying the density of visible lattice points on n-dimensional hyperplanes, as well as attempts to connect it with potential analogs of n-dimensional Farey Sequences and Stern-Brocot Diagrams.

This study quickly branched out into a study of the density of visible lattice points in general lattices. My longterm goal is now an adequate treatment/description of the density of visible lattice points in any integer lattice $\Lambda\simeq\mathbb{Z}^m$ which is an affine subspace of $\mathbb{Z}^n$. I also want to study all possible variations of visibility as well as the affect of linear transformations (matrices in GLnZ, GLmZ, other matrices with non unit determinants, translations by any point, reflections, rotations, etc). Furthermore, I want to study the affect of topological and algebraic operations on lattices.

Very quickly in my study of all this I became exposed to new fields of math, theorems, equations, techniques, applications, etc. It has become harder and harder to organize my thoughts, short term and long term goals, relationship of my work to the relevant literature, and all the notes I've made of things I really need to study/read up on. That is why I've made this obsidian vault, so I can properly organize and take care of my goals, my research, relevant papers, and all the new math I'm learning to be able to do this subject matter justice in my treatment of it!

Eventually, I would like this obsidian vault to serve as the basis of my senior thesis for my Yale math undergrad. Additionally, if I do it right I might be able to export all of my work here to html and eventually make a math blog page devoted to my study of this stuff. That can be complete with graphics, interactive lattice and visibility visualizers, and hyperlinks to all the great papers, texts, and MATH that I'm coming across in my exploration of lattices via their visibility.

Even in everything I come across, nobody has provided a thorough treatment of this beautiful topic that lies near the hearts of so many fields of math! I think this is really something special...

Honestly the current literature is all over the place, has anybody tried to bridge it all? I don't think so! This could honestly be my life's work...
