I spent the summer of 2024 researching the density of visible lattice on n-dimensional hyperplanes and their intersections.

If $M$ is a full rank $m\times n$ integral matrix I am interested in the set of visible points in the lattice spanned by $M$, that is $\{x\in\Z^n: gcd(Mx)=1\}$, specifically its asymptotic density in $\Z^n$. It's not hard to show that if $gcd(Mx)=1, gcd(x)=1$. So in other words, I'm interested in the density of the set of integral points whose visibility is invariant under the linear transformation $M$.

Since any matrix admits a smith normal form, we can restrict our attention to just diagonal matrices. What's cool is the only thing that matters are these invariant factors on the diagonal, so every lattice is identifiable with a finitely generated abelian group.

For simplicity's sake I start with the lattice $p\Z\times\Z$, which probabilistically and computationally I know has a density of $\frac{p}{p+1}\cdot\frac{1}{\zeta(2)}$




Though I haven't completed the proof yet, I believe I am capable of using standard sieve methods to prove the asymptotic density of any diagonal matrix.
